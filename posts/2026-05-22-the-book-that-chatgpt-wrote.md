A well-known author published a book using ChatGPT. It made headlines — not because the book was good, but because the AI had fabricated quotes and invented sources.

The author blamed the tool. Readers blamed the author.

But here's what nobody asked: what if the problem wasn't using AI, but *how* he used it?

There are two ways to work with AI. The first is delegation: "Write this for me." The second is collaboration: "Help me think this through."

Most people stop at delegation. That's the story that makes headlines.

When you ask AI to do everything, you get generic results. When you use it as a thinking partner — feeding it your ideas, debating with it, pushing back — something different emerges.

The author who blamed ChatGPT wasn't betrayed by a tool. He was betrayed by his own laziness.

Don't let AI replace your thinking. Let it sharpen it.
